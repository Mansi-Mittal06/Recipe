Rasmalai

Rasmalai is my favourite dish since my childhood.I am fond of sweet dishes,so I like almost every sweet dish.when me and my cousins oftenly went in weddings had compete with each other that who's gonna eat more and guess who won--me. ;-) Rasmalai is one of the popular and delicious Bengali sweet where rasgullas (cottage cheese balls) are soaked in thickened and sweetened milk.

Rasmalai contains two things:

  • Rasmalai balls
  • The thickened milk or the ras

Here are the recipe to make it:

Ingredients

For making rasmalai balls

  1. 1 liter whole milk
  2. 4 tablespoons lemon juice
  3. 1 teaspoon cornflour
  4. 4 cups water
  5. 1 cup sugar

For making thickened milk

  1. 500 ml whole milk
  2. 5-6 green cardamom pods peeled and crushed to get the powder
  3. saffron a pinch
  4. 3-4 tablespoons sugar
  5. finely chopped pistachios

Instructions

For making rasmalai balls:

  • Firstly, milk is boiled and then curdles by adding lemon juice or vinegar.ü•õ
  • After draining the water from the ccurdled milk a protein 'chena' left.ü§§

  • Small balls are then made from that chena and dropped in boiling sugar syrup till cooked.üòç

For making ras:

  • In a heavy bottom pan, boil 500 ml of milk. Soak few strands of saffron in a tablespoon of warm milk and set aside.ü•Ñ
  • Once the milk comes to a boil, lower the flame and continue to stir the milk at regular intervals. After 10 minutes add sugar and mix.
  • After 20-25 minutes the milk will thicken to desired consistency, add soaked saffron and crushed cardamom. Also add finely chopped pistachios [if using]. Mix and switch off the flame.üòã

Take out the cooled down rasmalai balls from thefresh water bowl. Squeeze and flatten lightly using your hands and put in sugar syrup for around 10-15 minutes.After 10-15 minutes transfer the balls to thickened milk. Chill in the refrigerator overnight or for 5-6 hours. Garnish with chopped pistachios and few saffron strands. before serving.
